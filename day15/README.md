# LifeLens ✦

A personal life analysis web app. Built for Day 15 of the **ABTalks 60-Day Claude AI Mastery Challenge**.

## Artifact Link
https://claude.ai/public/artifacts/6b7795d3-caa8-47af-81c5-7fc05e09110d

## The backstory

The original Day 15 prompt was a full Vedic astrology life reading tool — birth charts, Dasha periods, doshas, the whole Indian astrology system. I’m Christian, so I couldn’t personally relate to most of the prompt.

Looking past the astrology, the actual task was: *build a personal life analysis consultant.* The astrology was just the framework someone chose for it. So I rebuilt it on psychology, behavioral science, and life coaching frameworks instead, and made it usable by anyone regardless of faith, with an optional Christian/Biblical lens you can toggle on.

## What it does

You answer a short 3-step form:

1. Name, age, gender, relationship status
1. What you do, where you’re based
1. Your top 1-3 concerns, in your own words (work, love, money, identity, anything real)

LifeLens then gives you a tabbed analysis: your concerns addressed directly, an overview with a 5-area life radar chart, career & money outlook, relationships, a 5-year forecast, and a tiered action plan. Optionally, scripture and a prayer focus tied to your specific concerns.

## Why it’s not live-AI-generated

I originally built this to call Claude’s API live, in-browser, so every reading would be freshly generated by AI. After several days of debugging (JSON parsing failures, streaming response formats, intermittent backend errors, and the API requiring a signed-in Claude session for any visitor), I made the call to rebuild the analysis engine as a **fully self-contained, rule-based system** instead.

It’s not random. It reads what you actually type, classifies your concerns into categories (career, money, relationships, family, health, identity), and pulls from a library of real, specific insights, action steps, and scripture matched to those categories and your profession. It just isn’t AI-generated fresh text — that’s a planned future iteration.

## Tech stack

Pure HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript. No backend, no build step, no dependencies. One file.

## Run it

Open `lifelens.html` in any browser. That’s it.
