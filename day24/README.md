## What The Report Covers

### Business Reality Check

Six honest questions with honest answers:

- **Who pays?** Lagos SME vendors — not dispatch riders
- **Why do they pay?** Dispatcher dishonesty costs them money every week
- **How will they discover it?** Vendor WhatsApp groups and dispatch hub presence
- **Biggest growth risk?** Map data quality at launch
- **Biggest monetisation risk?** Freemium-to-paid conversion rate is unproven
- **Weakest assumption?** That vendors will pre-commit to paying before the MVP is built

### Business Model Canvas

Full nine-block canvas covering key partners, activities, value propositions, customer relationships, segments, resources, channels, cost structure, and revenue streams — structured for pre-MVP stage reality, not aspirational projections.

### Revenue and Pricing Strategy

Four pricing tiers validated against Nigerian market psychology:

| Tier | Price | Who |
|---|---|---|
| Free | ₦0/month | Dispatch riders — the network |
| Starter | ₦4,999/month | SME vendors with 1–3 riders |
| Growth | ₦12,999/month | Active vendors with 4–10 riders |
| Pro | ₦29,999/month | Logistics SMEs with 10+ riders |

Year 1 base case: ₦72M ARR (~$48K USD) from 500 Lagos vendors. Conservative and achievable without paid marketing.

Key insight: **₦4,999 sits deliberately below the ₦5,000 psychological resistance threshold.** One recovered package pays for six months of subscription. The ROI framing is the sales pitch — not the feature list.

### Go-To-Market Strategy

Five phases, sequenced by cost and risk:

1. **Seeding (Month 1–2):** Physical presence at Lagos dispatch hubs. Hand-install the app. No paid marketing.
2. **Word of mouth (Month 2–4):** One Vendor Vivian telling her WhatsApp group is worth ₦500,000 in advertising.
3. **Content (Month 2–6):** LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok. Build the brand as "the Nigerian GPS."
4. **Paid, light (Month 4+):** Instagram ads only after organic traction is confirmed.
5. **B2B Direct (Month 6+):** Outbound to logistics companies for Pro tier.

The key lesson here: **going big from the beginning wastes effort, time, and money.** The smarter path is seeding, learning, then scaling what is already working.

### Customer Acquisition Strategy

Separate acquisition strategies for vendors (paying customers) and riders (free network drivers):

- **Vendors:** WhatsApp DMs, Instagram outbound, dispatch hub presence, referral programme
- **Riders:** Physical installation at hubs, vendor mandates, rider-to-rider WhatsApp sharing

Target unit economics: CAC under ₦15,000 per vendor. LTV:CAC ratio above 6:1 at scale.

### First 100 Users Plan

Week-by-week breakdown:

- **Week 1:** 25 riders at dispatch hubs + 10 vendors via Instagram DMs
- **Week 2:** 25 more riders via vendor mandate + 10 more vendors via LinkedIn
- **Week 3:** 15 more via WhatsApp group posts
- **Week 4:** 25 more via rider-to-rider APK sharing

Definition of success at Week 3: 15 vendors complete 5+ tracked deliveries. That is the PMF signal.

### Competitive Position and Moat

| Competitor | Threat Level | Gap vs RouteIQ |
|---|---|---|
| Google Maps | Low (now), Medium (3yr) | No landmark routing, no dispatch tracking |
| Sendbox | Medium | No live rider location for vendors |
| Kwik Delivery | Low | Closed network — only their own riders |
| OkHi | Low-Medium | Address capture only, no navigation |
| New entrant (funded) | High (12–18 months) | First-mover data moat is the defence |

Moat stack ranked by defensibility: landmark map data (very high) → vendor workflow integration (high) → rider community trust (medium) → brand positioning (medium).

### Reverse SWOT Analysis

A standard SWOT tells you what you have. A Reverse SWOT asks: *what if our strengths are actually risks?*

- ML skills → risk of over-engineering before validating
- No incumbent → ask why before assuming the space is just overlooked
- Blank slate → advantage if used for research, not building
- 18% market growth → attracts well-funded competitors too
- Google Maps threat → actually unlikely; dispatch tracking is outside their scope entirely

## The 3-Gate Path to Fundable

Before any code:

```
Gate 1 → 20 user interviews (10 riders + 10 vendors)
Gate 2 → 5 vendors say YES to N4,999/month before MVP is built
Gate 3 → 7-day MVP build sprint (landmark sharing + live pin only)
```

Clear all three gates in 30 days → RouteIQ moves from promising concept to early-stage fundable business.

## Sustainability Verdict

RouteIQ Nigeria has the structural ingredients of a sustainable business. The problem is real and daily. The paying customer is identified and reachable. The network flywheel is logical. The founder has domain proximity, technical skills, and a proven shipping habit.

The business is not yet sustainable — no users, no revenue, no validated pricing. But the path to sustainability is unusually clear for an idea-stage startup. The single biggest risk between now and fundable is building before validating.

**Don't open a code editor until 10 user interviews are complete and 3 vendors have said yes.**
