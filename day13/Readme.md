## 💡 Key Learnings

### 1. AI sees what humans scroll past

After months of manual job hunting, the AI-assisted search — given precise criteria, structured constraints, and a clear profile — surfaced Satalia: a legitimately junior UK remote DS role with explicit mentorship and a tech stack match. This is the kind of result that gets buried under hundreds of senior postings when searching manually.

### 2. The skill gaps are real — but you’re already moving

Cloud (AWS) and LLMs (HuggingFace/Transformers) are the two highest-priority gaps across every market. Notably, you’re already on AWS Skill Builder. The LLM gap was a new callout and is now on the radar.

### 3. Platform matters as much as profile

Indeed is not the right primary channel for this profile and location. The same search on LinkedIn, Turing, or Andela would surface a significantly different (and more accessible) pool of roles. Where you search from shapes what you find.

### 4. The Nigerian barrier is real — but it’s a policy problem, not a skill problem

The job search confirmed what months of personal experience had already shown: qualified Nigerian candidates are routinely blocked not by their abilities but by hiring policies that restrict by nationality or work authorisation. Companies explicitly built for global hiring are the right target — not companies that tolerate it reluctantly.

### 5. Production systems speak louder than credentials

Every junior role that returned a strong match explicitly valued demonstrated projects over degrees or years of experience. A live forecasting system serving real humanitarian users is not a portfolio project — it is a production credential, and it should be treated and communicated as one.

### 6. Structured criteria produce better searches

Providing explicit job search criteria — preferred domains, salary targets, exclusions, soft requirements — allowed the AI to score and filter results against real-world constraints rather than returning a generic list. The structure is what made the output actionable.
