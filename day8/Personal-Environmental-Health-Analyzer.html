Radar: selected city vs. national average of 8 cities
Executive Summary — Nigeria, June 2026: Analysis of 8 major Nigerian cities reveals a national average AQI of 46, broadly placing the country in the Good-to-Moderate air quality range. However, intra-city variations are stark — Benin City leads as the cleanest city nationally (AQI 29), while Lagos records the highest pollution at AQI 74. Water quality tells a strikingly different story: Abuja's planned infrastructure yields the best water score (62/100), while Warri scores a critical 28/100 due to decades of Niger Delta oil-industry contamination. Most surprising: Kano — Nigeria's northern industrial and trade hub known for severe harmattan dust storms — currently registers only AQI 31, outperforming all southern cities. Residents of Lagos and Port Harcourt face compounding environmental health risks: above-average air pollution AND poor water quality simultaneously — a double burden that amplifies skin, hair, and systemic health impact.

Kano Beats All Southern Cities
Kano (AQI 31) outperforms Lagos, Warri, Port Harcourt, and Ibadan despite being Nigeria's most industrialized northern city — suggesting seasonal off-harmattan measurement.
★
Warri: Good Air, Crisis Water
Warri's water score (28/100) is the worst nationally. Oil spills across the Niger Delta have rendered groundwater critically unsafe while air quality remains deceptively acceptable.
↑
Abuja: Nigeria's Environmental Blueprint
Planned urban design delivers both AQI 35 (3rd cleanest) AND 62/100 water score (best nationally) — proving intentional infrastructure planning pays off in health outcomes.
