Data Provenance
Nutrition Data Sources
All RDA values, food composition data, and health thresholds are derived from peer-reviewed and government sources
USDA FoodData Central
fdc.nal.usda.gov
Primary global nutritional composition database maintained by the U.S. Department of Agriculture. Basis for all macro and micronutrient values.
ICMR-NIN Food Composition
icmr.gov.in · nin.res.in
Indian Council of Medical Research — National Institute of Nutrition tables for Indian-specific foods (Rice, Roti, Dal, Poha, Idli, Dosa, etc.).
Dietary Guidelines for Indians 2024
ICMR-NIN Publication
Updated macro ratios, micronutrient RDA values, and healthy eating plate guidelines for Indian adult populations.
NIH Office of Dietary Supplements
ods.od.nih.gov
Authoritative RDA, AI, and UL values for all tracked vitamins and minerals, including gender- and age-specific thresholds.
WHO/FAO Expert Consultations
who.int · fao.org
Global protein, energy, and micronutrient requirement standards. Reference for Omega-3 AI and Vitamin D recommendations.
Mifflin-St Jeor Equation (1990)
American Journal of Clinical Nutrition
Gold-standard BMR formula with ±10% accuracy vs indirect calorimetry. Used for all daily energy expenditure calculations in this tool.

Educational Disclaimer

NutriScope Pro is designed for educational and informational purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical, nutritional, or dietary advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

Nutritional values in this tool are approximate averages derived from reference databases. Actual nutrient content varies by food variety, ripeness, cooking method, preparation technique, regional differences, and brand. Values may differ from what you consume.

Daily targets are calculated using population-average formulas and reference values. Individual nutritional requirements are influenced by genetics, health conditions, medications, hormonal status, gut health, and other factors not captured by this tool.

Risk analysis flags are based on simple threshold comparisons from a single-day food log. They should not be interpreted as clinical assessments. If you have concerns about nutrient deficiencies, health risks, or dietary management for a medical condition, consult a registered dietitian (RD) or qualified healthcare professional.
