⚠ Racial Equity in Prior Authorization
Studies consistently show that Black patients face higher PA denial rates than white patients for identical procedures, even when controlling for clinical factors, insurance type, and income. For cardiac procedures, Black patients are denied at rates 20–35% higher than white patients with the same diagnosis. For psychiatric admissions, racial disparities compound the already-high baseline denial rate. PriorRight's equity screening layer addresses this directly — but the technology is only part of the solution. The law must name the disparity explicitly and enforce it.
Three Things You Can Do Right Now
1
Know your right to appeal. If you or someone you know receives a PA denial, request a formal appeal in writing immediately. 40–60% of denials are overturned on appeal. You also have the right to an Independent Medical Review through your state insurance commissioner — free and legally binding.
2
Ask your doctor about Peer-to-Peer Review. If your PA is denied, your treating physician can request a direct call with the insurance company's medical reviewer. This step overturns denials at a significantly higher rate than written appeals alone. Most patients don't know to ask for this.
3
Contact your elected representative. The GOLD CARD Act and the Improving Seniors' Timely Access to Care Act would significantly reform PA requirements. Write to your Senator or Representative and tell them this system is harming your family. Policy changes this significant only happen when people demand them.
