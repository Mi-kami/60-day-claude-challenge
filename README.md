## What This Is

This is a structured, public 60-day commitment to deeply mastering Claude and AI tools — not as a search engine, not as an autocomplete assistant, but as a genuine engineering and thinking partner.

The goal is not to collect certificates or finish tutorials. The goal is to come out on Day 60 with real fluency: in prompt engineering, in building AI-powered workflows, in applying Claude to production-level work, and in understanding how to think *with* AI at an architectural level.

Every day is documented. Every concept is tested against real work.

## 🎯 Challenge Objectives

By the end of 60 days, this challenge will have produced:

- A deep, practical understanding of how Claude thinks and responds
- Hands-on experience building real AI-powered tools and workflows
- A portfolio of documented prompts, systems, and mini-projects
- Fluency in moving from *chatting with AI* to *orchestrating AI*
- Applied skills directly transferable to an AI Engineering career track

-----

## ⚡ Six Learning Pillars

The 60 days are organized around six core areas. Each pillar builds on the last.

### 1 · AI Workflows

Designing and executing multi-step AI processes that go beyond single prompts. Learning how to structure tasks so Claude can handle them with minimal back-and-forth. Moving from reactive prompting to deliberate workflow design.

### 2 · Prompt Engineering

The craft of communicating precisely with AI. This covers system prompts, few-shot examples, chain-of-thought, role assignment, instruction formatting, and everything in between. Not just *what* to say — but *why it works*.

### 3 · Automation

Using Claude and AI tooling to eliminate repetitive cognitive tasks. Connecting Claude to pipelines, building reusable templates, and integrating AI into existing work systems. Making automation that actually holds up.

### 4 · AI Productivity

Applying AI to real daily work — research, writing, coding, analysis, planning. This is about finding where AI creates genuine leverage and building habits around it. Not productivity theater. Actual output improvement.

### 5 · Context Engineering

Understanding how Claude processes context — token windows, memory management, conversation structure, and role priming. Learning to give Claude exactly the context it needs and nothing it doesn’t. This is the difference between good prompts and great systems.

### 6 · Real-World Projects

Everything comes together in applied builds. Mini-projects and challenge outputs that use Claude in production-like scenarios — data pipelines, document processing, analysis workflows, agentic tasks. If it can’t be deployed, it doesn’t count.
